# MANDATE: THE DISTRICT GAME
## A three-player political strategy card game

---

## 1. THE CORE IDEA

**MANDATE: The District Game** is a three-player abstract political strategy game.

Players represent three political positions:
- the **Left**,
- the **Right**,
- and the **Independents**.

The game is not about ideology, but about **influence and decision-making power**.  
Players compete to secure **Districts** at the right moment, using the right tools.

The first player to secure **three Districts** wins a **round**.  
The game is played as a **Best of 3 (BO3)** â€“ first to win **2 rounds** wins the game.

## 4. SETUP

1. Place the **7 Districts** in a row in the center of the table.
2. Shuffle the full deck.
3. Deal **6 cards** to each player.
4. Place the remaining cards face down as the draw pile.
5. Randomly determine the starting player.

---

## 5. DISTRICT STRUCTURE

- A player may only place cards on **their own side** of a District.
- Each player may place **up to 3 cards** at a District.
- A District may therefore contain **up to 9 cards** total.

## 10. CLAIMING A DISTRICT

Districts are **automatically claimed** when:

- **At least two players** have completed configurations (3 cards each)  
**OR**
- Any player has played a **Total Mandate (AAA)**

### Resolution:
- The strongest configuration wins the District
- The District is **claimed and closed**
- No further cards may be played there
- The turn continues automatically

---

## 11. WINNING A ROUND

> The first player to claim **three Districts** wins the **round**.

---

## 12. BEST OF 3 (BO3) FORMAT

### Round Structure:
- **Round 1:** Independents start first
- **Round 2:** Left starts first  
- **Round 3:** Right starts first

### Winning the Game:
- First player to win **2 rounds** wins the game
- If all 3 players win 1 round each (1-1-1) â†’ **Tiebreaker**

### Tiebreaker Order:
1. **Total Districts won** across all 3 rounds (highest wins)
2. **Best Configuration achieved** (Total Mandate > Color Run > etc.)
